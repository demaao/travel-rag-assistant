Vatican Visit - My Experience

During my stay in Rome, I visited Vatican City, and it was one of the most impressive places I saw during my trip. Even though it is very small, it felt extremely important and powerful because of its history and religious significance.

The main place I visited was St. Peter’s Basilica. When I first saw it from the outside, I was surprised by how large and detailed it was. Inside, the space felt even more impressive. The ceilings were very high, and there were many beautiful sculptures and decorations. I could tell that a lot of effort and time was invested in every detail.

What I liked:
- The incredible architecture and size of the basilica
- The artistic details and decorations inside
- The unique atmosphere compared to other places in Rome

What I didn’t like:
- The lines were very long, and I had to wait a lot before entering
- The area was very crowded, especially during the middle of the day

Experience:
Walking inside the basilica felt different from visiting other attractions. It was quieter, and people behaved more respectfully. It gave me a feeling that this place is not only a tourist site but also very meaningful to many people.

Problems:
- Waiting in line under the sun was uncomfortable
- It was hard to move freely inside because of the crowd

Personal feeling:
The Vatican was one of the most beautiful places I saw, but also one of the most exhausting because of the crowds.

Tips:
- Come very early in the morning to avoid long lines
- Dress properly (cover shoulders and knees) to enter religious sites
- Be patient and prepared for crowds
- Take your time inside and look at the details

Milan Trip - My Experience

During my trip to Italy, Milan felt very different from the other cities I visited. While cities like Rome and Florence were full of history and old buildings, Milan felt much more modern and organized. It gave me the feeling of being in a business and fashion center rather than a historical city.

One of the main things I did in Milan was walk around the shopping district. I saw many luxury stores and famous brands, and the streets looked very clean and well designed. Even though I didn’t buy anything, it was interesting to see how important fashion is in this city.

What I liked:
- The clean and organized environment
- The modern atmosphere
- Wide streets and easy navigation

What I didn’t like:
- Everything felt very expensive, especially in the shopping areas
- The city had less of the traditional “Italian” feeling compared to places like Rome or Florence

Personal feeling:
Milan felt more like a place for work, shopping, and modern lifestyle rather than tourism focused on history. It was interesting, but I didn’t feel the same emotional connection I had in other cities.

Food experience:
I ate in a café near the city center. The food was good, but it didn’t feel as special as the meals I had in Naples or Rome.

Tips:
- Visit Milan if you are interested in fashion or shopping
- Don’t expect a strong historical atmosphere
- Be prepared for higher prices
- Spend a short time there compared to other cities

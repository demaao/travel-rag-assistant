Rome Trip - My Personal Experience

I visited Rome during my trip to Italy, and it was one of the most interesting and memorable cities I experienced. The city felt like a mix of history and modern life, where every street had something special to offer.

The place that impressed me the most was the Colosseum. When I arrived, the line was very long, and I had to wait almost an hour under the sun. At first, it was a bit frustrating, but once I finally entered, I understood why it was so popular. The structure is massive, and walking inside it made me imagine how it looked during ancient times with thousands of people watching events.

I also spent time walking around the city center. I visited the Trevi Fountain, which was very crowded but still beautiful. Throwing a coin into the fountain felt like a fun tradition. Walking through the old streets was one of my favorite parts, even though it was tiring.

What I liked:
- The rich history and unique atmosphere
- Exploring the old streets and discovering hidden places
- Seeing famous landmarks like the Colosseum and Trevi Fountain

What I didn’t like:
- Too many tourists in popular areas
- Long waiting times for attractions
- Crowded streets, especially in the afternoon

Food experience:
I ate pasta carbonara in a small local restaurant. It tasted much better than anything I had before. The flavor was simple but very rich and authentic.

Problems:
- The weather was very hot, which made walking harder
- Walking long distances became tiring after a few hours

Personal feeling:
Rome felt exciting but also exhausting. There was always something to see, but it required a lot of energy.

Tips:
- Book tickets in advance to avoid long lines
- Bring water, especially in hot weather
- Wear comfortable shoes for long walks
- Visit popular places early in the morning
